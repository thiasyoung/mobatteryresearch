Partners

Industry, universities, and development organizations working together.

The legacy site positioned MOCAP as a network rather than a single institution. Industry partners, public universities, incubator resources, and regional organizations all contribute to the program's research and education mission.

Partnership model

University research, industrial application, and economic development aligned around advanced power technologies.

University partners

Academic institutions contributing curriculum, faculty, and research depth.

University of Missouri - Columbia

The legacy site described the University of Missouri as the first public university west of the Mississippi and the flagship campus of the four-campus University of Missouri System.

MOCAP highlighted MU's research reputation, breadth of academic programs, and role in advanced power coursework and electrochemical instruction.

Missouri State University - Springfield

Missouri State was presented as a comprehensive institution with undergraduate, graduate, and professional doctoral programs, plus a strong emphasis on public affairs, teaching, and collaboration.

  • Named to Forbes America's Top Colleges
  • Best in the Midwest distinction from The Princeton Review
  • Regional Universities recognition from U.S. News & World Report
  • Military Friendly School recognition from G.I. Jobs magazine

Missouri S&T - Rolla

Missouri S&T was framed as a top technological research university with a history dating to 1870 and a global reputation for engineering education and innovation.

  • No. 1 “Best Investment” among public universities for out-of-state students (legacy site citation)
  • No. 1 “Awesome College Lab” for the Experimental Mine (legacy citation)
  • High starting salaries among public university graduates (legacy citation)

Missouri Southern State University - Joplin

Missouri Southern hosts the MOCAP lab and was described as a four-year liberal arts public institution serving nearly 6,000 students across more than 200 academic programs.

The Chemical and Physical Sciences Department was identified as an active governance partner, with students able to support research as assistants or interns.

Industry and regional organizations

Organizations tied to application, commercialization, and economic growth.

EaglePicher Technologies, LLC

EaglePicher was described as a premier manufacturer of cell, battery, and power system solutions serving defense, space, aircraft, medical, and commercial markets.

The legacy site emphasized the company's interest in tailored employee education and research partnerships tied to new battery materials, processes, and manufacturability.

NorthStar Batteries - Joplin

NorthStar Batteries was presented as a Missouri-based manufacturer of thin-plate absorbed glass mat lead acid batteries serving telecom, UPS, transportation, and grid-energy storage markets.

The site described NorthStar as a local industry partner interested in collaborative electrochemical research and long-term workforce development.

Joseph Newman Innovation Center

The Innovation Center was identified as a Missouri Certified Business Incubator with a mission to create a positive environment for entrepreneurs, especially those pursuing innovative technology.

Joplin Area Chamber of Commerce

The Chamber was described as a membership organization focused on economic prosperity and quality of life in the Joplin region, and as an advocate for the business community.

Missouri Technology Corporation

MTC was described as a public-private partnership created by the Missouri General Assembly to promote entrepreneurship and foster new and emerging high-tech companies.

The legacy page highlighted its vision to transform Missouri through entrepreneurship and technology-based innovation.
