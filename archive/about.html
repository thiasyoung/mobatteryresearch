About MOCAP

A Missouri partnership built around advanced power research.

MOCAP was created to connect research, public investment, private industry, and workforce development around battery and power storage technologies. The partnership spans the Tech-44 Corridor and central Missouri.

Statewide reach

University partners, industry collaborators, and regional development groups operating across Missouri.

Overview

Research development plus workforce preparation.

The legacy site described MOCAP as a public-private effort supported by the Missouri Legislature and the Missouri Department of Economic Development to expand opportunities in battery and power storage devices.

With six partners located in Missouri's Tech-44 Corridor and one in central Missouri, MOCAP is devoted to research and development of new battery and advanced power technologies while educating a new high-tech workforce for the future.

Missouri is positioned as a leader in research and development across agriculture, manufacturing, life sciences, and engineering. MOCAP's niche is advanced power storage: helping researchers test emerging chemistries while helping students and professionals build relevant skills.

One of the partnership's strengths is proximity to EaglePicher Technologies in Joplin. The organization highlights that connection as a way to provide technical assistance and practical relevance for researchers working in electrochemistry and battery systems.

Public-private model

MOCAP ties together universities, private companies, and economic development organizations rather than operating as a single-campus lab or a stand-alone company.

Missouri focus

The original copy consistently frames MOCAP as a statewide asset intended to strengthen Missouri research and support future industry growth.

Practical assistance

Researchers can use the facility, seek technical support, and connect with faculty across multiple institutions working in related fields.

Core priorities

The three themes repeated across the original site.

Research and development

Battery chemistry, electrochemical devices, power storage, and product testing all sit at the center of the MOCAP message.

Workforce education

Students and working professionals are expected to benefit from classroom instruction, distance learning, certificates, and applied facility experience.

Statewide coordination

MOCAP is presented as a connector between local facilities in Joplin and faculty expertise spread across Missouri's higher-education network.

“Missouri is becoming a leader in research and development across many fields. MOCAP provides a unique role in 21st century battery and power storage and educating students that will solve the power requirements of the future.”

Statement attributed on the legacy site to Missouri Governor Jay Nixon.
